Nick Botting

Nick Botting exhibits every year at the Portland Gallery in London.

We think we have some of his best paintings here in Carlton. Some were painted last time he visited Australia and include Windy Day, Airey’s Inlet, The Dome Reading Room, State Library of Victoria and some Carlton views England Going Out to Bat after Lunch 1st Test, Lahore 2000.

Nick Botting is a well-known English painter who loves to paint ‘en plein air’. He has been known to set up his easel anywhere from the middle of an intersection to the roof of a car and once carried a full length mirror out into the Australian desert to paint a self-portrait! His paintings are concerned with capturing small moments in time and preserving them, and the people in them, for the viewer’s voyeuristic pleasure.

Biography:
1963 Born in England
1977 - 1981 Inspired by his art teacher Graeme Drewe (Australian) at Winchester College
1981 - 1982 Foundation course at Herfordshire College of Art and Design
1983 - 1986 University of Kent, BA Hons, Visual and Performed Arts
1984 Solo exhibition at Gulbenkian Theatre, University of Kent
1991 - 1999 Seven solo exhibitions at Barkes and Barkes, London
1997 Exhibited with Royal Institute of Oil Painters, Mall Galleries, London, Roberson Award Winner
1998 Solo exhibition at Savill Galleries, Sydney
1999 & 2005 Solo exhibitions at McKenzie Gallery, Perth
1999 National Portrait Gallery, London, BP Portrait Award, Highly Commended
2000 - 2001 Exhibitions at Catto Gallery, London
2000 Official artist for the English cricket team on tour in Pakistan
2001 Solo exhibition at Lord's Museum, London. Portrait commissions of Ian Botham and also H.M. the Queen with the Australian cricket team (both now hanging in Lord's Museum)
2001 Invited to exhibit with the New English Art Club, Mall Galleries, London
2003 - 2009 Annual exhibitions at Portland Gallery, London
2007 & 2009 Solo exhibitions at Evarard Read Gallery, South Africa
2007 Appointed by the FA as its official artist; commissioned to paint first FA Cup Final to be played at the new Wembley Stadium, London
2008 Discerning Eye Exhibition, Mall Galleries, London, Founder's Prize Winner

Collections:
MCC Museum, Melbourne, FA Headquarters, London; Lord’s Cricket Museum, London; Really Useful Theatre Group; Duchy of Lancaster; Mall Galleries; Andrew Lloyd Webber, Ian Botham & Lord Salisbury
